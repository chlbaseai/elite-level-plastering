Prompt 3 — Scroll Video

The Scroll Loop

Fixed low three-quarter overhead camera matching both reference frames, 3-second transition at 30fps. Resting state: mixing bucket full, all tools arranged around it on the concrete floor, everything still and grounded. On scroll-forward: the wet plaster surface inside the bucket bulges upward then bursts — the heavy mass of plaster rises first as a single cohesive blob before breaking into trailing splatters. Simultaneously the trowel and hawk lift and begin to rotate slowly. Then medium items follow — the corner bead uncoils and spirals upward, the plaster bag tears wider and powder erupts in a soft expanding cloud, the spirit level drifts sideways while rotating. The mixing paddle rises vertically, shedding plaster in slow drips. Last to move: the small details — pencil, box-cutter, plaster crumbs, scrim tape unfurling, dust particles blooming into the air, water droplets releasing from the sponge float in a slow-motion scatter. Peak state: full suspension, maximum spread, every object frozen at its highest point with gentle rotational drift. Physics style: low-gravity float with viscous plaster behavior — the wet plaster moves heavily and stretches before breaking apart, while dry items float lighter and faster. On scroll-reverse: dust and particles pull back first like magnetic filings, then small tools glide inward, medium items compress and align, finally the plaster mass descends and settles back into the bucket with a slight surface ripple, tools lower to their resting positions with a soft bounce-settle. Camera remains perfectly static throughout. Seamless bidirectional loop.
