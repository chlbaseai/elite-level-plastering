Prompt 3 — Transformation Video

The 3-Second Render

Fixed three-quarter front camera matching both reference frames, 3-second video at 30fps, time-lapse speed. Opening frame: the house in full exposed red brick, still and quiet. At 0.2 seconds a plasterer enters frame-right wearing grey work trousers and a dark t-shirt, carrying a loaded hawk in his left hand and a steel trowel in his right. He moves at extreme fast-forward speed — arms working in rapid fluid strokes, spreading cream-coloured render across the brick face of the ground floor. The render appears in smooth sweeping bands following his trowel, each stroke covering a wide area. The red brick vanishes panel by panel under wet cream plaster that quickly sets to a smooth matt finish. At 1.2 seconds the ground floor is fully rendered, the plasterer moves to a scaffold platform that appears in fast-forward assembly and begins the first floor. His movements are continuous, rhythmic, and confident — no hesitation, no stopping. Wet plaster catches the overcast daylight with a slight sheen before drying flat. Small details at speed: plaster dripping from the trowel edge, the hawk being reloaded from a bucket, the plasterer stepping sideways along the scaffold. At 2.5 seconds the final section around the upper window is completed in rapid precise strokes. The plasterer steps down and exits frame-left. At 2.8 seconds the scaffold disappears in fast-reverse. Final frame at 3 seconds: the fully rendered cream house, still and clean, matching the after photograph exactly. Camera remains perfectly static throughout. Natural overcast lighting consistent across the entire sequence. The speed should feel satisfyingly fast — like watching a master craftsman on 50x fast-forward.
